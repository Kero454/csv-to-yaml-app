Step 1: Experiment Name - Help
What is this step for?

This is the first step in creating your machine learning experiment. Here you give your experiment a unique, descriptive name that will help you identify and organize your work.

Experiment Name Guidelines:
  • Be Descriptive: Use names that clearly indicate the purpose (e.g., "Customer_Churn_Prediction")
  • Include Context: Add dates, versions, or dataset info (e.g., "Sales_Forecast_2024_V2")
  • Use Underscores: Replace spaces with underscores for better file compatibility
  • Avoid Special Characters: Stick to letters, numbers, and underscores
What happens next?

After naming your experiment, you'll proceed through these steps:

  1. Configuration: Upload or create configuration files
  2. Data: Upload your CSV datasets
  3. Architecture: Define your model architecture
  4. Optimization: Configure hyperparameter sweeping (optional)
Pro Tip: Choose a name you'll remember and that describes your experiment's goal. You can always create multiple experiments with similar names but different versions.
